# Team Meeting 1 Notes

**Date:** 17/06/2026  
**Project:** DataLens Streaming  
**Client:** StreamFlix  
**Meeting purpose:** Initial team kickoff following the client briefing and early project scoping

This first team meeting was used to align on the client brief, confirm the project goal, discuss team responsibilities, and identify the immediate questions that needed to be sent back to the client. The project is focused on supporting StreamFlix as it transitions from a free, ad-supported platform to a subscription-based service through data-driven analysis and dashboard reporting.

## Client brief summary

The client explained that StreamFlix needs to decide which film genres and titles to prioritise as part of its platform revamp. The aim is to retain current users, attract new subscribers, and support the company’s global growth strategy using objective data rather than assumptions.

The client specifically asked for insights into:
- what types of films different groups of users enjoy based on age, location, and subscription type,
- which genres and movies are most successful for each audience segment,
- and a dashboard with clear visualisations that stakeholders can interpret easily.

The timeline is very tight. The client requested a first version of the project proposal by **1 July**, with the broader platform relaunch expected only a few weeks later.

## Team understanding of the project

The team agreed that the project purpose is to help StreamFlix transition successfully to a subscription model by identifying which genres and films should be prioritised for acquisition and promotion. The expected deliverable for the first phase is a data-driven dashboard supported by actionable insights and a clear project roadmap.

The team also recognised that the work will depend heavily on the quality and structure of the available data, especially user demographics, subscription information, watch behaviour, genre classification, and film performance measures.

## Team decisions

During the meeting, the team agreed on the initial project coordination structure, including:
- Scrum Master: Sabahat Naz
- Jira management: Shanika Kodithuwakku
- GitHub/repository maintenance: Andrew Chung

These responsibilities were assigned so that sprint coordination, task tracking, and version control would each have a clear owner from the beginning of the project.

## Key discussion points

The team discussed the urgency of the project and the importance of narrowing the work to a realistic first version before 1 July. It was agreed that the first iteration should prioritise business-relevant insights that can be clearly explained to stakeholders rather than trying to answer every possible question at once.

The team also discussed likely data needs and possible risks. Early concerns included whether the client’s data would be provided through flat files or database access, whether the film catalogue was already mapped by genre, and how “success” should be measured for movies and genres.

## Clarifying questions identified

Following the meeting, the team prepared a set of clarification questions for the client to reduce uncertainty and support project planning. These questions focused on:

1. **Data access and structure**
- Whether demographic, subscription, and watch history data would be provided as CSV files or through direct database access.
- Whether StreamFlix already has a consolidated film inventory mapped to genres.

2. **Definition of success**
- Whether success should be measured through watch time, completion rate, ratings, or another metric.
- Whether the client already uses performance bands or expects the team to define them.
- Whether rights acquisition costs and historical promotional spend are available.

3. **Strategy and stakeholders**
- Whether there are priority regions to focus on for the first iteration.
- Who the dashboard is intended for, such as executives, content acquisition teams, or marketing stakeholders, and what level of detail they need.

## Client follow-up

After the meeting, the team sent a clarification email to the client covering data access, success metrics, priority regions, and dashboard stakeholders. This follow-up was intended to support the project outline and reduce uncertainty before the next phase of planning.

## Immediate actions agreed

The team agreed to:
- finalise the project outline,
- document the initial scope in the repository,
- send clarification questions to the client,
- set up the project workflow through Jira and GitHub,
- and begin preparing for the data analysis and dashboard design stages.

## Next steps

The next phase of work will depend on the client’s response to the clarification questions and the availability of the required datasets. Once the data environment is confirmed, the team will move into detailed planning, data review, dashboard design, and preparation of the first project version due on 1 July.
