# Client Needs
*Created based on alignment outcomes during [Team Meeting 01](../sprint-notes/team-meeting-01.md).*

## 1. Client Information and Project Overview

- **Client:** StreamFlix.
- **Project name and purpose:** The company is transitioning from a free, ad-supported platform to a subscription-based model, with the goal of identifying which film genres to prioritize to satisfy existing users and attract new subscribers.

## 2. Objectives of the Solution

### Key questions the client wants answered
- Which film genres should be prioritized to satisfy existing users and attract new subscribers?
- What types of films are enjoyed by different user groups based on age, location, and subscription type?
- Which genres and movies are the most successful for each specific audience?

### Main goals for the platform
- Successfully transition the company from a free, ad-supported platform to a subscription-based service.
- Prioritize film purchases that existing users appreciate while simultaneously attracting new subscribers.
- Use objective data to support the company’s global growth ambitions.

### Types of insights needed
- Insights segmented by age, location, and subscription type to understand film preferences.
- Performance data identifying successful genres and specific movies for different audience segments.

### Expected outputs
- Actionable insights derived from objective data to guide business decisions.
- A Power BI dashboard featuring clear, easily interpretable visualizations for stakeholders.
- The first version of the project proposal by July 1st.

## 3. Data Requirements

### Types of data involved
- User demographic information, specifically age and location.
- User subscription data, to distinguish between different types of users.
- Film preference and engagement data, to identify which films and genres are enjoyed by specific user groups.
- Performance metrics regarding successful genres and individual movies.

### Format and availability of data
- The current format or method of access has not been specified.

### Gaps or limitations in the data
- Not enough data has been provided yet to determine current data gaps or technical limitations.
- A key immediate step is to clarify the availability and quality of these datasets because the timeline is very tight.

## 4. Required Timeframe and Project Constraints

### Timeframe
- The project timeline is very tight.
- The first version of the project must be proposed by July 1st.

### Project constraints
- The revamped platform has already been publicly announced, creating pressure for the project launch.
- The platform must be ready to launch in a few weeks after the project proposal.
- The analysis must be based on objective data to support decisions for global growth.

## 5. Technical Prerequisites and Deliverables

### Reporting tools to be used
- The client requests a dashboard with clear visualizations that stakeholders can easily interpret.
- The team has prior experience using Excel, Power BI, and PowerPoint for this type of deliverable.

### Data analysis tools/languages/applications to be used
- The team has prior experience with SQL, pandas, Excel, and Power BI for data wrangling and analysis.

## 6. Circumstances That Make the Task Urgent or Special

### Client priorities
- Prioritizing film acquisitions that satisfy existing users while also attracting new subscribers.
- Transitioning the business model from a free, ad-supported platform to a subscription-based service.
- Using objective data to guide strategic decisions as the company pursues global growth.

### What makes the project urgent or unique
- The project timeline is very tight.
- The company has already publicly announced the revamped platform, which is scheduled to launch in a few weeks.
- There is a hard deadline to propose the first version of the project by July 1st.

## 7. Anticipated Difficulties or Obstacles

### Potential difficulties mentioned
- The project timeline is very tight.
- The revamped platform has already been publicly announced, creating a strict deadline for launch.
- The project requires objective, data-driven insights to guide global growth decisions.

### Questions to clarify challenges
- What is the current state and accessibility of the data needed to analyze user preferences by age, location, and subscription type?
- Are there any technical or data limitations that could prevent delivery of the first version by the July 1st deadline?

## 8. Other Questions to Ask

- **Data availability and quality:** Are the datasets for user demographics, subscription types, and watch history already consolidated, or will they need to be integrated from multiple sources? Is there also data on subtitle and audio availability in different languages?

- **Defining success:** How does StreamFlix define a “successful” movie or genre? For example, is success measured by watch time, completion rate, user ratings, or another performance metric?

- **Global expansion priorities:** Since the company wants global growth, are there specific regions or markets that should be prioritised in the first version of the analysis?

- **Stakeholder requirements:** Who are the main stakeholders for the dashboard, what decisions do they want to make from the insights, and is the audience mainly technical or non-technical?

- **Content inventory:** Does the company already have a complete inventory of its film library, and is that inventory mapped to the genres being analysed?

- **Platform constraints:** Are there any technical constraints in the revamped platform or related systems that could affect how dashboard data is displayed, shared, or consumed?

- **Rights cost data:** Is there data on the cost of acquiring rights for different film genres?

- **Actions from insights:** Will the results be used to guide film purchases, promotions, or both, and will cost considerations be part of those decisions?
